This document synthesizes findings from the trend analysis, competitor profiles, and best practice benchmarking into actionable strategic implications for ABC. ABC currently scores 25/60ESTIMATE (2.1/5 average) on the competitive scorecard -- the lowest among all profiled competitors. 6.2 Most Dangerous Competitor: Home Credit

Home Credit is the most dangerous competitor for ABC because:

  1. It is winning on every metric that matters: Lowest NPL (1.4%), highest profitability (ROAE 30.5%), strongest growth (19.3%), most innovative (BNPL, EV livestream, TikTok marketing)
  2. Its BNPL solution is embedded in Vietnam's largest retail chain (MWG/The Gioi Di Dong) -- ABC's BNPL (when launched) will compete against an entrenched incumbent
  3. It targets a similar Mass/Lower Mass segment as ABC, creating direct competition for the same customers
  4. It is demonstrating that disciplined risk management and growth are not mutually exclusive -- challenging the narrative that high growth requires high NPL

6.3 Most Analogous Model: HD Saison

HD Saison's ecosystem model (HDBank + VietJet + SkyJoy) is the closest analogue to ABC's ABC ecosystem potential. Key lessons:

  • Ecosystem integration takes time (HD Saison started in 2013, SkyJoy maturity reached ~2023-2024)
  • The customer graduation pathway (FinCo customer becomes bank customer) adds lifetime value
  • Loyalty platforms are the glue that binds ecosystem participants
  • Physical touchpoints (50K+ for HDBank/HD Saison/VietJet combined) create distribution advantage
  • Cross-entity data sharing enables superior credit scoring

6.4 Most Disruptive Threat: Super Apps

MoMoiMoMo — 51M Users, Vietnam Leading E-Wallet (40M+ users), ZaloPayiZaloPay — VNG Corporation Digital Wallet (Zalo's 75M+ users), and Viettel Money (70M+ subscribers) represent the long-term disintermediation risk. As these platforms accumulate transaction data and build credit capabilities, they could:

  • Offer instant micro-credit directly, bypassing FinCos
  • Partner selectively with banks (not FinCos) for regulatory cover
  • Build proprietary credit scoring superior to FinCos' bureau-dependent models
  • Compress FinCo margins by controlling customer access

ABC's defense: The ABC ecosystem provides independent customer access that super apps cannot disintermediate. ABC Mall shoppers, MaxValu grocery buyers, and MiniStop convenience store visitors are ABC's captive audience, reachable without super app intermediation.
