3. Regulatory Environment

3.1 SBV Policy Direction

The State Bank of Vietnam is pursuing a dual mandate of supporting economic growth while maintaining financial stability:

  • Accommodative monetary policy: Refinancing rate held at 4.5% (historic low) since June 2023
  • Credit growth target: ~16%IKIGAI PROJECTION for 2025, with flexibility to adjust based on inflation
  • Financial inclusion mandate: Target 80% adult bank account ownership by end 2025
  • Digital transformation: Open API regulation (Circular 64/2024) effective March 1, 2025
  • Regulatory sandbox: Decree 94/2025 for fintech/P2P lending innovation
  • Consumer protection: Stricter penalties on forced insurance cross-selling (draft decree replacing 88/2019/ND-CP)

3.6 VNBA Proposal to Raise Consumer Loan Cap

The Vietnam Banks Association has proposed key reforms to boost FinCo competitiveness:

  • Raise unsecured loan cap: From VND 100 million to VND 300-400 million for finance companies
  • Adjust direct disbursement threshold: From VND 20 million to VND 40 million exemption; recognize e-wallet/bank disbursement as non-cash
  • Revise portfolio requirement: Reduce minimum consumer credit share from 70% (Circular 35/2024) to 50%, or replace with risk-based prudential indicators
  • Impact: Enables FinCos to serve higher-income segments, diversify products, compete more effectively with banks and P2P platforms

3.7 Debt Collection Regulation

  • Law on Investment 2020: Third-party debt collection classified as "strictly prohibited" business activity (effective January 2021)
  • Decree 98/2020/ND-CP: Fine of VND 60-80 million for operating in prohibited debt collection sectors
  • Impact on FinCos: Must rely entirely on internal collection, increasing delinquency risk; encouraging investment in AI-driven early warning systems and digital repayment channels
