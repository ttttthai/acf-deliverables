1. Embedded Finance and Ecosystem-Driven Lending

1.1 The Shift from Direct to Embedded Distribution

The traditional model of consumer finance -- direct sales agents at point of sale, standalone branches, and field-based origination -- is being systematically replaced by embedded finance models where credit is distributed through partner ecosystems.

In Vietnam's consumer finance market, B2Bc (Business-to-Business-to-Consumer) lending has become the dominant customer acquisition strategy. FinCos execute this by embedding credit into partner ecosystems including banks, retailers, fintechs, and telcos. The rationale is clear: intensifying competition and rising customer acquisition costs make partnerships an increasingly imperative channel.

1.2 Four Partnership Archetypes in Vietnam

According to FiinGroup's 1H2025 analysis, FinCos deploy partnership strategies across four archetypes:

Banks (Archetype 1): Three bank-backed FinCos (HD Saison/HDBank, MCredit/MB, FE Credit/VPBank) leverage banking ecosystem synergies. They inherit product development capabilities, big data exploitation, credit scoring, and reduced cost of funds. HD Saison exemplifies this with its SkyJoy loyalty platform connecting 14+ million members across HDBank, HD Saison, and VietJet Air ecosystems.

Retailers (Archetype 2): FinCos partner with electronics retailers (The Gioi Di Dong, FPT Shop, CellphoneS), e-commerce platforms (Lazada, Tiki, ShopeeiShopee — SEA E-Commerce Platform, Embedded Finance), and e-wallet ecosystems (Viettel Money, ZaloPayiZaloPay — VNG Corporation Digital Wallet, VNPAY). These channels offer consumer durables installment lending, BNPL with hidden fees and prepayment, and financial products via online interfaces.

Fintechs (Archetype 3): Fintechs like Payoo, MoMoiMoMo — 51M Users, Vietnam Leading E-Wallet, and Traveloka offer FinCos access to young customers without credit history. FinCos exploit the young customer bases, leverage consumption behaviors from fintechs, and promote financial products via in-app advertisements.

Telcos (Archetype 4): Partnerships with Viettel and Vietnam Post leverage (1) countrywide distribution reaching remote areas, (2) strong brand trust, and (3) telco employees as potential customers with verified profiles and stable income.

2. Super Apps and Digital Payment Disruption

2.1 The Rise of Vietnam's Super App Ecosystem

iMoMo — 51M Users, Vietnam Leading E-Wallet Vietnam's digital payment landscape is dominated by three super app ecosystems that are rapidly embedding financial services:

MoMo: Vietnam's largest e-wallet with 40+ million users. MoMo has expanded beyond payment intermediation to encompass savings deposits, fund certificates, microloans, and embedded BNPiZaloPay — VNG Corporation Digital WalletL. In November 2023, MoMo partnered with Grab to enable BNPL for Grab's services, further extending its credit footprint.

ZaloPay: Backed by VNG Corporation and leveraging Zalo's 75+ million user messaging platform, ZaloPay integrates BNPL options through partnerships with FinCos including Lotte Finance and TNEX Finance.

Viettel Money: Leveraging Viettel's dominant telco position (70+ million subscribers), Viettel Money integrates financial services including BNPL (in partnership with FE Credit since 2021) and postpaid payments.

2.2 Market Size and Payment Penetration

Vietnam's mobile payments market has experienced explosive growth. For online shopping in 2024, payment methods break down as: Cash on Delivery (COD) 67%ESTIMATE, e-wallet 18%, credit card 8%, debit card/transfers 5%, and BNPL 2%. While BNPL's share remains small, its trajectory is steep as e-commerce penetration exceeds 9.9% of total retail sales.

The e-commerce market has grown at a 23.5% CAGRESTIMATE from 2022-2024, reaching approximately USD 25 billion, with top 10 players controlling 95%ESTIMATE of market share. By 2025, Vietnam is projected to have 85+ million internet users (84%+ penetration).

2.3 Threat Assessment for ACF

Super apps represent both an opportunity (as distribution partners) and a threat (as potential disintermediation channels). ABC's current lack of a robust digital app and documented eKYC drop-off issues leave it vulnerable. Competitors like VietCredit have doubled their portfolio through aggressive digital channel push, achieving 89.4%ESTIMATE YTD growth in 1H2025.

Key concern: As super apps build credit scoring capabilities based on transaction data, they could bypass FinCos entirely, relegating traditional consumer finance companies to capital provision roles with compressed margins.

ABC is notably absent from the EV financing landscape. Given ABC Group's global sustainability commitments and ABC's pipeline inclusion of 2-wheeler loans, this represents both a gap and an opportunity.

5. M&A Activity and Market Consolidation

5.1 Recent M&A Transactions

Vietnam's consumer finance sector has seen significant M&A activity as foreign players seek entry and existing players consolidate:

  • ABC Financial Services acquired PTF (Post and Telecom Finance) fully in 2025, expanding its Vietnam consumer finance footprint through a dedicated cash loan and installment entity
  • HDBank acquired SGVF (Societe Generale Viet Finance) in 2013, creating HD Saison as one of Vietnam's top three consumer finance companies
  • VPBank retained full ownership of FE Credit after SMBC's investment, continuing to leverage its largest subsidiary
  • Lotte Finance raised charter capital to VND 4.9 trillion (USD 188.3M) in 2025 with SBV approval, signaling aggressive expansion plans

5.2 Potential M&A Targets and Scenarios

The sector remains fragmented with several smaller players (SHB Finance, JACCS, Toyota Financial Services, Easy Credit) that could be acquisition targets. SHB Finance in particular shows distress signals with negative PBT, ROAA of -1.1%, and ROAE of -11.9%.

5.3 ABC's M&A Positioning

The acquisition of PTF positions ABC uniquely. The combined ACS Trading Vietnam (installment payments, 700,000 members) and PTF (cash loans, 200,000 members) gives ABC a broader product portfolio. However, the EY assessment reveals significant integration risks: PTF's infrastructure is at capacity, cybersecurity controls are weak, and there is no customer-facing app for loan <span style="position:relative;display:inline-block;cursor:help;margin-left:3px" title="State Bank of Vietnam (sbv.gov.vn)"Executive Summary Report_v.3.1.pptx" — IT Security Assessment">iorigination and repayment.

6.4 Impact on ACF

ABC faces a dual digital threat: bank encroachment from above and fintech disruption from below. The EY assessment identifies that ABC (AFS-PTF) currently has no customer-facing mobile app for loan origination and repayments, manual processes in the Newgen LOS lead to errors, and there is no database encryption or real-time SIEM monitoring. These gaps are existential in an increasingly digital competitive landscape.

7. AI Adoption and Advanced Analytics

7.1 AI in Credit Scoring -- Global Benchmarks

Global best practices from leaders like Nubanki"UBank and FE Credit Proposal_with pricing_20251128.pdf" OtherData/WeBank (70+ million customers using ML-based scoring), Ant Financial (Zhima/Sesame Credit serving 1+ billion users), and Grab Financial demonstrate how AI transforms consumer lending. Key applications include:

  • Real-time credit decisioning: Instant loan approval using ML models processing hundreds of data points in milliseconds
  • Dynamic pricing: Personalized interest rates based on real-time risk assessment

  • Generative AI: Creating synthetic data for stress testing and risk modeling, simulating economic scenarios beyond historical experience

Vietnamese FinCos are at varying stages of AI adoption:

  • FE Credit: Deployed AI chatbot supporting 130,000+ users monthly, Big Data analytics platform for credit scoring, FE ONLINE 2.0 as comprehensive digital platform
  • Home Credit: Leveraging AI and digitalization to consolidate customer base, using data-driven approaches for TikTok and livestream marketing campaigns
  • VietCredit: Digital-first strategy driving 89.4% portfolio growth through enhanced digital customer experience and credit scoring
  • HD Saison: Partnership with Credit Saison Japan for credit scoring and risk management technology transfer
  • MCredit: API-based loan approval in under 2 minutes via MB Bank ecosystem

7.3 ABC's AI Readiness

Per the EY assessment, ABC's current technology stack presents significant limitations for AI adoption:

  • Data architecture relies on Oracle-based ODS/DWH on SeABankiABC Banking Partner Agreement — Disbursement Channel infrastructure (legacy from PTF)
  • No independent data lake or feature store for ML model development
  • Data visualization limited to PowerBI and Excel
  • No advanced analytics capabilities (AI/ML) currently deployed
  • Data governance framework is absent, creating data quality issues

The EY roadmap recommends implementing a Medallion Architecture pattern (Bronze/Silver/Gold data layers), establishing an independent data lake with AI capabilities for forecasting and predictive analysis, and migrating to a modern cloud-based data platform. These foundational investments are prerequisites for competitive AI-powered credit scoring.

  • HD Saison's limitations on cash loan disbursement

10. Trend Convergence and Strategic Outlook

10.1 Trend Convergence Map

The seven trends are not independent -- they converge to create a fundamentally different competitive landscape:

  1. Embedded finance + Super apps = Credit becomes invisible, distributed at point of need
  2. BNPL + E-commerce growth = New micro-credit segment worth USD 8.5B by 2030
  3. ESG/EV + Regulatory support = Green lending as differentiation opportunity
  4. AI + Digital banking = Real-time, personalized credit decisions as baseline expectation
  5. M&A + Bank encroachment = Consolidation accelerates, scale becomes survival threshold
  6. Regulatory modernization + Product evolution = Higher ticket sizes, new product categories

10.2 Five-Year Outlook (2026-2030)

Near-term (2026-2027):

  • BNPL market exceeds USD 4 billion
  • EV financing becomes mainstream as Hanoi fossil fuel ban takes effect (Jan 2026)
  • Regulatory sandbox produces first licensed P2P and digital lending platforms
  • Credit card issuance grows 50%+ annually
  • ABC launches credit card program leveraging WAON ecosystem

Medium-term (2028-2029):

  • Super apps embed full lending capabilities, pressuring standalone FinCos
  • AI-powered credit scoring becomes table stakes
  • ESG reporting requirements formalized for financial institutions
  • FinCo consolidation reduces active players from 16 to 10-12

Long-term (2030):

  • Vietnam consumer finance market exceeds VND 25,000 trillion
  • Digital-first lenders dominate new origination
  • AEON targets Top 3 credit card issuer position
  • Green lending portfolio reaches 15-20% of consumer finance
