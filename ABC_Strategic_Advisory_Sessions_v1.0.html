Advisory Session
Session 1: Customer Acquisition at Scale

Context Briefing

ABC faces a fundamental acquisition challenge: 67% of current disbursements come from telesales (overwhelmingly ACS cross-sell to existing ABC installment customers), 33% from a declining NSN dealer network with severe quality issues (MediaMart at 52.9% FPD, Cho Lon suspended), and less than 1% from digital channels. The addressable base for ACS cross-sell is capped at approximately 200,000 customers from the existing ABC installment portfolio. Meanwhile, FE Credit has 14M+ customers, Home Credit is growing at 19.3% YTD with 26,000+ retail touchpoints, and MCredit achieves 2-minute loan approval through MB Bank's ecosystem.

ABC's current customer base is approximately 50,000 active accounts. The target is 800,000+ within 3 years. That is a 16x scaling challenge.

Critical insight: this acquisition challenge must be solved along two fundamentally different tracks, because ABC Mall customers and cash loan customers are different people.

Track A (ABC Ecosystem -- Installment/BNPL): ABC Mall shoppers are mass/upper-mass, educated, employed, urban, and banked. They are ideal for installment loans on electronics, furniture, and lifestyle purchases at ABC, as well as future BNPL and credit card products. WAON silent scoring is the primary acquisition engine for this track. These customers walk into ABC -- the question is converting them from shoppers into borrowers.

Track B (Open Market -- Cash Loans): Cash loan customers are lower-mass, blue-collar, factory workers, gig economy participants, and semi-urban populations. They generally do NOT shop at ABC Mall. They are underbanked, have thin CIC files, and need cash for emergencies, rent, motorbike repairs, and family expenses. Acquiring these customers requires going outside the ABC ecosystem entirely -- to industrial zones, employer partnerships, telco channels, and digital outreach.

The core question is not just "where do we find 750,000 new customers?" -- it is "how do we build two separate acquisition engines for two fundamentally different customer segments?"

Key Questions Explored

  1. How does the two-track reality change acquisition strategy? ABC walk-ins should be upsold on installment products and BNPL (Track A), not cash loans. Cash loan acquisition (Track B) requires entirely separate channels -- industrial zones, telesales to non-ABC populations, digital marketing, employer partnerships, and telco distribution.

  2. Which micro-segments represent the cash loan opportunity (Track B)? Factory workers in industrial zones (4M workers, VND 7-12M/month), gig economy workers (Grab, Be, delivery riders), small-stall merchants in traditional markets, and semi-urban workers in emerging industrial corridors (Binh Duong, Dong Nai, Long An, Bac Ninh). These populations are underserved by FE Credit and Home Credit and do not shop at ABC Mall.

  3. Can WAON loyalty data create a zero-CAC acquisition channel for installment products (Track A)? Yes. WAON purchase data (frequency, recency, basket size, category stability) can predict creditworthiness with accuracy comparable to traditional bureau scoring for thin-file segments. However, WAON scoring is specifically an installment/BNPL play -- it pre-qualifies ABC shoppers for ABC purchases, not for cash loans.

  4. What is realistic about employer partnerships for cash loan acquisition (Track B)? Vietnam has 400+ industrial zones employing approximately 4 million workers. Binh Duong and Dong Nai alone host 1M+ workers. Most lack access to formal consumer credit because they have thin CIC files and irregular documentation, despite earning VND 7-12M/month. These workers are the core cash loan target -- they need cash, not installment credit at ABC Mall.

  5. Are telco partnerships realistic for cash loan distribution (Track B)? FE Credit already partners with Viettel Money for BNPL. However, a data-for-distribution exchange model (as opposed to a simple distribution agreement) has not been executed in Vietnam's consumer finance market. Viettel's 77M subscribers include millions of lower-mass customers who are ideal for cash loans. This represents a genuine first-mover opportunity for ABC's Track B growth.

Discussion and Analysis

Track B: The Industrial Zone Opportunity -- Cash Loan White Space

Vietnam's industrial zones represent the largest underserved cash loan market in Southeast Asia relative to worker income. An estimated 4M workers earn VND 7-12M/month but have thin credit files because: (a) they are often migrants from rural provinces, (b) they receive wages via cash or informal bank transfers, (c) they rarely interact with formal financial products beyond basic savings accounts. FE Credit and Home Credit target them opportunistically through retail POS at electronics stores near industrial zones, but neither has built structured employer partnerships.

Critically, these workers are the ideal cash loan customer -- not ABC Mall shoppers. They need cash for rent, motorbike payments, family remittances, and emergencies. They do not need installment credit for electronics at ABC Mall (they cannot afford ABC Mall prices). This distinction is essential: industrial zone targeting is a Track B (cash loan) acquisition strategy, not a Track A (installment) strategy.

ABC has a proximity advantage: ABC Citimart, MiniStop, and MaxValu locations are positioned near key industrial corridors in Binh Duong (ABC Mall Binh Duong, plus convenience stores), Dong Nai, and Long An. While these workers may shop at Citimart for groceries, they are not ABC Mall lifestyle shoppers. The connection with ABC convenience stores can serve as a physical touchpoint for cash loan origination and repayment, but the product sold must be cash loans, not installment credit.

The model: ABC signs MoU with factory HR departments to (a) verify employment and salary, (b) offer salary-advance and cash loan products with repayment via payroll deduction. The employer benefits because it reduces advance-request administrative burden and provides a worker welfare benefit that aids retention. Disbursement goes to bank accounts or e-wallets, not WAON store credit.

Track A: WAON Silent Scoring -- The Installment Acquisition Engine

Every competitor relies on CIC bureau data, which has a GINI coefficient of only 26-27% for ABC's current scorecard. This means it barely distinguishes good from bad borrowers in the Mass/Lower Mass segment. The reason: CIC is built on formal credit history, but many ABC shoppers -- especially younger customers -- have limited formal credit history despite being creditworthy.

WAON data fills this gap for Track A customers. A customer who buys groceries at Citimart every Tuesday and Friday for 12 months, spending VND 200-400K per visit, with consistent basket composition (rice, vegetables, milk), is demonstrating financial stability that no CIC score captures. Initial analysis suggests WAON-based behavioral features could improve model GINI by 8-12 percentage points for thin-file applicants.

WAON scoring is specifically an installment/BNPL pre-approval tool, not a cash loan acquisition channel. The customers it identifies are ABC shoppers -- mass/upper-mass, urban, employed. The right offer for these customers is: "You are pre-approved for a VND 20M installment on any ABC purchase" or "0% interest on your next electronics purchase at ABC." Offering cash loans to WAON-scored customers mispositions the product to a segment that does not need it and has bank access for cash needs.

The acquisition flow: ABC runs the scoring model across all 5M WAON members nightly. The top 20% (1M members) receive pre-approved installment and BNPL offers. These offers are delivered at the ABC checkout counter via the WAON app or printed on the receipt. The customer can activate the offer by scanning a QR code. CAC: near zero. Risk: lower than any new-to-bank channel because the scoring is based on months of observed behavior.

Cash Loan Acquisition (Track B): Digital, Employer, and Telco Channels

For Track B (cash loans), digital marketing and partner channels are the primary acquisition engines -- not ABC walk-in traffic:

  • Telesales to non-ABC populations: Current ACS cross-sell exhausts the ABC installment base. Cash loan telesales must target purchased lead lists from employer databases, telco partners, and digital lead generation -- reaching people who have never set foot in an ABC Mall.
  • Employer partnerships: Factory HR-linked origination is the highest-quality cash loan acquisition channel. Payroll data provides income verification that replaces thin CIC files. Target: 10 factory partnerships providing access to 50,000+ potential cash loan borrowers.
  • Gig platform partnerships: Grab, Be, Gojek, AhaMove, and delivery platforms employ hundreds of thousands of gig workers who need short-term cash. Their platform data (ride frequency, earnings consistency, rating) serves as alternative credit scoring for cash loans.
  • Telco distribution (Viettel): 77M Viettel subscribers include millions of lower-mass customers ideal for cash loans. Mobile behavior data (top-up frequency, data consumption) provides alternative scoring. Viettel Money app provides distribution to customers who never visit ABC.

Digital marketing has different economics by track. For Track A (installment), ABC owned media is effectively free. For Track B (cash loans), paid digital is necessary but must be carefully managed:

  • CPA inflation: Facebook CPM in Vietnam finance vertical has increased 40%+ since 2023. FE Credit, Home Credit, and MCredit collectively spend VND 200-400B/year on digital ads.
  • Trust deficit: ABC has near-zero brand awareness for cash lending. The ABC Mall brand does not help here -- cash loan customers do not associate ABC with lending.
  • The implication: Track B digital marketing must build a separate trust identity (financial safety net, dignity-preserving emergency credit) rather than riding the ABC retail brand.

Advisory Session
Session 2: Disbursement & Repayment UX

Context Briefing

Vietnam's payment landscape is undergoing rapid transformation: VietQR transactions grew 81% YoY, MoMo has 51M users, and cashless transactions hit 5.5 billion in Q1 2025 alone. Yet paradoxically, COD still dominates e-commerce at 67%, and auto-debit penetration remains extremely low. ABC's current disbursement is slow (bank transfer, sometimes 24-48 hours) and repayment options are limited.

The two-track reality fundamentally changes disbursement and repayment strategy:

  • Track A (Installment/BNPL) customers shop at ABC regularly. ABC POS repayment, WAON store credit disbursement, and in-store QR payment are natural for this segment. These customers can "Pay While They Shop" because they are already shopping at ABC.
  • Track B (Cash Loan) customers do NOT regularly visit ABC Mall. They need cash disbursed to bank accounts or e-wallets (MoMo, ZaloPay), and they need repayment channels where they actually are: convenience stores (Circle K, FamilyMart, GS25), VietQR at any bank, e-wallet payment, and payroll deduction. Building ABC POS repayment for cash loan customers is solving the wrong problem.

Key Questions Explored

  1. What does "instant" mean for each track? For Track A (installment customer at ABC Mall), "instant" means WAON card credit and walking out with goods. For Track B (cash loan customer), "instant" means funds in their MoMo or bank account within minutes -- they are not standing in an ABC Mall.

  2. Why is auto-debit adoption so low in Vietnam, and what can ABC do differently? Vietnamese consumers distrust auto-debit because of historical overcharging incidents and because many Mass-segment workers have irregular income timing. ABC cannot rely on auto-debit as a primary collection mechanism. For Track B (cash loan), payroll deduction is the superior alternative to auto-debit.

  3. Can ABC retail locations serve as repayment infrastructure? Yes -- but only for Track A (installment/BNPL) customers who already shop at ABC. For Track B (cash loan), repayment infrastructure must be built on convenience stores, VietQR, and e-wallets.

  4. How does repayment UX affect delinquency? Research from Home Credit and FE Credit shows that 30-40% of early-stage delinquency is caused not by inability to pay but by friction in the payment process. The Track A solution (ABC POS) and Track B solution (convenience stores, VietQR, e-wallets) both address this friction but through fundamentally different channels.

Discussion and Analysis

Track A: "Pay While You Shop" -- For Installment Customers Only

The insight that transforms Track A collection strategy: every installment borrower is also an ABC shopper. ABC knows which borrowers shop at ABC because it has WAON data. If an installment borrower is 5 days past due and shops at Citimart on Saturday morning, ABC can trigger a gentle in-app reminder at the point of purchase: "Add VND 300K to your loan payment today?" with a one-tap QR code at the checkout.

This converts a traditionally adversarial interaction (debt collection) into a seamless, frictionless, non-confrontational moment. The customer is already spending money at ABC -- adding a loan payment is psychologically easy because the payment context is "shopping," not "debt."

This only works for Track A (installment/BNPL) customers who regularly shop at ABC. Cash loan customers (Track B) do not regularly visit ABC Mall. For Track B, ABC must build a different repayment infrastructure: convenience store payment (Circle K, FamilyMart, GS25 -- ubiquitous in industrial areas and semi-urban locations), VietQR at any bank or payment terminal, e-wallet payment (MoMo, ZaloPay), and payroll deduction for salary-linked loans.

Track A: ABC Store Credit as Installment Disbursement -- The Closed-Loop Model

The fastest disbursement in Vietnam's consumer finance market is currently MCredit's 2-minute API-based approval through MB Bank. But even after approval, funds take 15-60 minutes to arrive in a bank account. For a customer standing in ABC Mall who wants to buy a VND 15M washing machine, those 15-60 minutes feel like an eternity.

ABC can offer something faster for installment customers: approve the installment loan in 3 minutes, credit the amount to the customer's WAON card instantly, and the customer pays at the ABC checkout register immediately. Total time from application to purchase: under 5 minutes.

This is not just faster -- it is structurally different. The disbursement never leaves the ABC ecosystem. The credit is usable only at ABC stores, which means: (a) lower fraud risk (no cash-out schemes), (b) higher purchase intent (customer is already in-store with a specific product in mind), (c) ecosystem lock-in (every loan drives ABC retail revenue).

This is exclusively a Track A innovation. Cash loan customers need cash -- disbursement to bank accounts or e-wallets is the only viable approach for Track B. The salary advance model for factory workers (Track B) should disburse to bank accounts or MoMo/ZaloPay, not WAON store credit.

Prioritized Recommendations

2.1 "Pay While You Shop" Repayment at ABC POS
PRIORITY 1 -- BUILD | TRACK A (INSTALLMENT)

Rationale: 30-40% of early delinquency is friction-driven, not ability-driven. Embedding repayment into the weekly ABC shopping trip removes friction entirely for Track A (installment/BNPL) customers who are already ABC regulars. ABC is the only FinCo in Vietnam that owns the retail POS where its installment borrowers regularly transact. This single innovation could reduce B1 delinquency by 10-15 percentage points for the installment portfolio. Note: this does NOT apply to Track B (cash loan) customers, who do not regularly shop at ABC. Cash loan repayment requires separate infrastructure (see Rec 2.4).

Implementation:

  • Month 1-2: Build technical integration between ABC loan management system and ABC POS/payment system. Enable "installment loan payment" as a line item on ABC checkout receipts.
  • Month 3: Pilot at 3 ABC Mall locations. Customer scans QR code at checkout, selects payment amount, confirms. Payment processes alongside grocery purchase.
  • Month 4-5: Deploy at all ABC stores (malls, supermarkets, minimarts). Integrate with WAON app for reminder notifications when customer enters ABC store (geofenced push notification).
  • Month 6-12: Introduce "auto-round-up" feature: customer opts in to round up every ABC purchase to the nearest VND 10,000, with the difference applied to their installment loan balance.

Quick Win vs Build: BUILD (3-month technical development, but unique sustainable advantage)

Dependencies: ABC POS system integration (requires ABC Vietnam IT cooperation), QR payment infrastructure, geofencing capability in WAON/ABC app.

Risks: Technical complexity of POS integration across multiple store formats. Mitigation: start with QR-based standalone flow (not POS-integrated) for MVP; integrate into POS in Phase 2.

First Action Step: Schedule technical scoping meeting with ABC Vietnam IT and POS vendor within 2 weeks. Define MVP requirements for QR-based repayment at checkout.

2.2 Instant ABC Store Credit Disbursement
PRIORITY 1 -- BUILD | TRACK A (INSTALLMENT)

Rationale: For installment loans originated at ABC stores, disbursement to a bank account is unnecessary friction. The customer wants to buy a product at ABC -- credit the WAON card instantly. This achieves sub-60-second disbursement (vs 15-60 minutes for bank transfers), eliminates cash-out fraud, and keeps 100% of loan proceeds within the ABC ecosystem (driving group revenue). This is a pure Track A innovation -- it only works for customers making ABC purchases. Cash loan customers (Track B) need cash disbursed to bank accounts or e-wallets; WAON store credit is not useful to them.

Implementation:

  • Month 1-3: Build WAON card credit functionality (instant balance top-up upon loan approval). Integrate with LOOMS loan disbursement workflow.
  • Month 4: Pilot at ABC Mall HCMC for electronics installment purchases. Customer applies via Sales App at the store, receives approval in 3 minutes, WAON card is credited, customer pays at register.
  • Month 5-6: Extend to all ABC retail locations for installment/BNPL products. Do not apply to cash loan disbursement -- cash loan customers need funds in their bank account or e-wallet, not store credit.

Quick Win vs Build: BUILD (requires WAON card payment infrastructure enhancement)

Dependencies: WAON card system must support instant credit loading, ABC POS must accept WAON card as payment method for high-value transactions.

Risks: Regulatory: SBV may require that loan proceeds be disbursed to a bank account (current interpretation of Circular 43/2016 is ambiguous for closed-loop payments). Mitigation: structure as "direct merchant payment" rather than "disbursement to customer."

First Action Step: Engage ABC Financial Service (parent) legal team to assess regulatory pathway for WAON card-based loan disbursement under Vietnamese law.

2.3 Behavioral Nudge Repayment Architecture
PRIORITY 2 -- QUICK WIN | BOTH TRACKS

Rationale: Vietnamese consumers in the Mass segment respond strongly to social proof and loss aversion. Rather than generic "your payment is due" SMS, ABC should deploy behaviorally-calibrated repayment nudges: "94% of ABC members like you paid on time this month" (social proof), "Pay today and earn 500 WAON points" (positive incentive), or "Your ABC Member status may be affected" (loss aversion for WAON benefits).

Implementation:

  • Month 1 (Quick Win): A/B test 5 different repayment reminder messages via SMS and Zalo for customers 3 days before due date. Measure payment rate by message variant. Baseline: current generic reminder achieves approximately 55% on-time payment.
  • Month 2: Deploy winning message variants across all channels. Introduce WAON point incentive for on-time payment (100 WAON points per on-time payment = VND 1,000 cost to ABC, vs VND 50-100K collection cost per delinquent account).
  • Month 3-6: Build automated nudge sequence: Day -3 (positive reminder), Day 0 (due date reminder with WAON incentive), Day +1 (gentle follow-up), Day +3 (loss-framed message about WAON status), Day +7 (restructuring offer).

Quick Win vs Build: QUICK WIN (A/B test deployable in 2 weeks)

Dependencies: SMS/Zalo messaging capability (existing), WAON point integration for incentives, A/B testing framework.

Risks: Over-messaging may annoy customers and increase Zalo OA unsubscribe rates. Mitigation: cap total messages at 4 per payment cycle; allow customer opt-out.

First Action Step: Design 5 message variants this week. Set up A/B test in SMS platform for next payment cycle. Select 10,000 customers for test group.

2.4 Multi-Rail Disbursement & Repayment for Cash Loan Customers
PRIORITY 1 -- BUILD | TRACK B (CASH LOAN)

Rationale: Cash loan customers (Track B) -- factory workers, gig economy, blue-collar -- disproportionately use e-wallets over bank transfers for daily transactions. MoMo has 51M users; ZaloPay is growing rapidly among the 25-40 demographic. Unlike Track A (installment) customers who receive WAON store credit and repay at ABC POS, Track B customers need both disbursement and repayment channels outside the ABC ecosystem. Disbursement to MoMo/ZaloPay is perceived as "instant." Repayment must be available at convenience stores (Circle K, FamilyMart, GS25 -- ubiquitous near industrial zones and semi-urban areas), via VietQR, and through e-wallet auto-deduction. This recommendation is elevated to Priority 1 because it is essential infrastructure for scaling cash loan volume.

Implementation:

  • Month 1-2: Integrate MoMo disbursement API. Enable "Disburse to MoMo" as primary option for cash loans under VND 20M. Simultaneously integrate convenience store repayment network (Payoo or equivalent) enabling cash loan payment at 15,000+ convenience store and payment agent locations.
  • Month 3-4: Add ZaloPay disbursement. Introduce customer choice screen: "Where should we send your money? MoMo / ZaloPay / Bank Account." Enable VietQR-based repayment at any bank or payment terminal. Build salary auto-deduction for employer-linked cash loans (Track B core product).
  • Month 5-6: Enable split disbursement: customer can direct 50% to MoMo for immediate use and 50% to bank account for savings. Deploy repayment reminder with nearest convenience store location via Zalo/SMS.

Quick Win vs Build: BUILD (2-3 months for API integration)

Dependencies: MoMo and ZaloPay partner API agreements, KYC alignment between ABC and e-wallet providers, regulatory compliance for third-party disbursement channels.

Risks: E-wallet disbursement may be perceived as less secure than bank transfers by some customers. Mitigation: offer as option, not default; position as "fastest way to receive your funds."

First Action Step: Initiate commercial discussions with MoMo business development team. Request API documentation and integration timeline.

2.5 VPBank NEO Agent Banking: Cash Disbursement & Repayment at ABC and MaxValue Locations
PRIORITY 1 -- BUILD | BOTH TRACKS

Rationale: VPBank NEO agent banking solves the most persistent friction point in ABC's cash loan business: many Track B customers are underbanked and prefer cash. They do not have bank accounts for electronic disbursement, and they distrust auto-debit. VPBank NEO agent banking at ABC Mall and MaxValue Store locations enables instant cash disbursement (customer gets approved, walks to the agent banking counter, walks out with cash) and cash repayment collection (customer pays their ABC installment in cash while shopping). No bank account needed for either transaction. This extends the "Pay While You Shop" concept (Rec 2.1) through formal banking infrastructure -- it is not limited to ABC POS but operates through a regulated agent banking channel. For Track A (installment) customers, VPBank NEO at ABC Malls adds a cash disbursement option alongside WAON store credit -- useful for customers who want to make purchases at non-ABC merchants with installment loan proceeds. For Track B (cash loan) customers, this is transformative: instant physical cash disbursement at MaxValue Stores in residential neighborhoods and at VNPost locations in semi-urban areas.

Implementation:

  • Month 1-2: Define disbursement and repayment workflows with VPBank NEO. Cash disbursement: ABC approves loan, sends disbursement instruction to VPBank NEO, customer receives cash at agent banking counter using OTP verification. Cash repayment: customer walks in, provides loan number, pays cash, receives receipt -- ABC system updated in real-time.
  • Month 3-4: Pilot cash disbursement and repayment at 5 ABC Mall VPBank NEO counters. Measure: time-to-cash (target: under 10 minutes from approval to cash in hand), customer satisfaction, repayment collection volume.
