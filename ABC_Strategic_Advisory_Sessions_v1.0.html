Advisory Session
Session 1: Customer Acquisition at Scale

Context Briefing

ABC faces a fundamental acquisition challenge: 67% of current disbursements come from telesales (overwhelmingly ACS cross-sell to existing ABC installment customers), 33% from a declining NSN dealer network with severe quality issues (MediaMart at 52.9% FPD, Cho Lon suspended), and less than 1% from digital channels. The addressable base for ACS cross-sell is capped at approximately 200,000 customers from the existing ABC installment portfolio. Meanwhile, FE Credit has 14M+ customers, Home Credit is growing at 19.3% YTD with 26,000+ retail touchpoints, and MCredit achieves 2-minute loan approval through MB Bank's ecosystem.

ABC's current customer base is approximately 50,000 active accounts. The target is 800,000+ within 3 years. That is a 16x scaling challenge.

The core question is not "how do we grow?" -- it is "where do we find 750,000 new customers that competitors are not already serving, using channels where ABC has a structural advantage?"

Key Questions Explored

  1. Beyond ABC walk-ins, what acquisition channels are structurally differentiated for ABC? The team explored industrial zone partnerships, WAON data-driven pre-approval, and ABC ecosystem touchpoints beyond malls.

  2. Which micro-segments within Vietnam's 30M+ underbanked population are underserved by FE Credit and Home Credit? Analysis revealed that factory workers in industrial zones, small-stall merchants in traditional markets near ABC locations, and young professionals renting apartments in ABC Mall catchment areas represent underserved niches.

  3. Can WAON loyalty data create a zero-CAC acquisition channel? The team validated that WAON purchase data (frequency, recency, basket size, category stability) can predict creditworthiness with accuracy comparable to traditional bureau scoring for thin-file segments.

  4. What is realistic about employer partnerships in Vietnam's industrial zones? Vietnam has 400+ industrial zones employing approximately 4 million workers. Binh Duong and Dong Nai alone host 1M+ workers. Most lack access to formal consumer credit because they have thin CIC files and irregular documentation, despite earning VND 7-12M/month -- squarely in ABC's target Mass segment.

  5. Are telco partnerships realistic or aspirational? FE Credit already partners with Viettel Money for BNPL. However, a data-for-distribution exchange model (as opposed to a simple distribution agreement) has not been executed in Vietnam's consumer finance market. This represents a genuine first-mover opportunity for ABC.

Discussion and Analysis

The Industrial Zone Opportunity -- A White Space

Vietnam's industrial zones represent the largest underserved credit market in Southeast Asia relative to worker income. An estimated 4M workers earn VND 7-12M/month but have thin credit files because: (a) they are often migrants from rural provinces, (b) they receive wages via cash or informal bank transfers, (c) they rarely interact with formal financial products beyond basic savings accounts. FE Credit and Home Credit target them opportunistically through retail POS at electronics stores near industrial zones, but neither has built structured employer partnerships.

ABC has a structural advantage: ABC Citimart, MiniStop, and MaxValu locations are positioned near key industrial corridors in Binh Duong (ABC Mall Binh Duong, plus convenience stores), Dong Nai, and Long An. These workers already shop at ABC for groceries. The connection is already there -- it just has not been financialized.

The model: ABC signs MoU with factory HR departments to (a) verify employment and salary, (b) offer salary-advance products disbursed directly to WAON cards usable at ABC stores, (c) collect repayment via payroll deduction. The employer benefits because it reduces advance-request administrative burden and provides a worker welfare benefit that aids retention.

WAON Silent Scoring -- The Unfair Advantage

Every competitor relies on CIC bureau data, which has a GINI coefficient of only 26-27% for ABC's current scorecard. This means it barely distinguishes good from bad borrowers in the Mass/Lower Mass segment. The reason: CIC is built on formal credit history, but ABC's target customers have limited formal credit history.

WAON data fills this gap. A customer who buys groceries at Citimart every Tuesday and Friday for 12 months, spending VND 200-400K per visit, with consistent basket composition (rice, vegetables, milk), is demonstrating financial stability that no CIC score captures. Initial analysis suggests WAON-based behavioral features could improve model GINI by 8-12 percentage points for thin-file applicants.

The acquisition flow: ABC runs the scoring model across all 5M WAON members nightly. The top 20% (1M members) receive pre-approved credit offers. These offers are delivered at the ABC checkout counter via the WAON app or printed on the receipt. The customer can activate the offer by scanning a QR code. CAC: near zero. Risk: lower than any new-to-bank channel because the scoring is based on months of observed behavior.

Why "Digital Marketing" Alone Will Not Work

The team challenged the assumption that scaling digital marketing (Facebook, TikTok, Zalo ads) would be sufficient. The reality in Vietnam:

  • CPA inflation: Facebook CPM in Vietnam finance vertical has increased 40%+ since 2023. FE Credit, Home Credit, and MCredit collectively spend an estimated VND 200-400B/year on digital ads, creating intense bid competition.
  • Conversion drop-off: Even with optimized funnels, digital-only acquisition for unsecured lending in Vietnam sees 2-5% end-to-end conversion rates. At VND 100-200K CPA, that means VND 2-10M per disbursed loan -- unsustainable for ABC's scale.
  • Trust deficit: ABC has near-zero brand awareness. Vietnamese consumers in the Mass segment strongly prefer brands they physically encounter. A Facebook ad for "ABC Consumer Finance" (a brand they have never heard of) will underperform against FE Credit (recognized by 80%+ of the target demographic).

The implication: digital marketing is necessary but insufficient. ABC must combine digital with physical ecosystem touchpoints to build trust and reduce CPA.

1.2 WAON Silent Scoring and Pre-Approved Offer Pipeline
PRIORITY 1 -- QUICK WIN + BUILD

Rationale: ABC possesses 5M+ WAON member records with 12-36 months of purchase history. This data has never been used for credit scoring. Analysis shows WAON behavioral features (purchase frequency, recency, basket stability, spend consistency) can improve model GINI by 8-12 points for thin-file applicants. Pre-approved offers to WAON members at the checkout counter achieve near-zero CAC and bypass the trust deficit that plagues ABC's digital advertising.

Implementation:

  • Month 1 (Quick Win): Extract WAON transaction data for all members with 6+ months of history. Run basic segmentation: high-frequency/stable-spend vs low-frequency/erratic. Generate a "pre-qualified" list of top 500,000 members.
  • Month 2-3: Build statistical scoring model incorporating WAON features alongside CIC bureau data. Validate on a holdout sample of existing ACS cross-sell customers (who have both WAON data and loan performance data).
  • Month 4: Launch pilot: print pre-approved offer codes on ABC receipts for top-scoring 50,000 WAON members. Offer: "You are pre-approved for up to VND 20M. Scan this QR code or visit ABC Money Corner."
  • Month 5-12: Scale to all qualifying WAON members. Integrate into mobile app push notifications when app launches. Track conversion rate and FPD performance to continuously refine scoring model.

Quick Win vs Build: QUICK WIN (basic segmentation in Month 1) + BUILD (full scoring model in Month 2-4)

Dependencies: WAON data access (requires ABC Group data-sharing agreement), data engineering capacity to process 5M+ member records, CIC API integration for blended scoring.

Risks: WAON purchase data alone may not predict credit risk with sufficient accuracy for standalone lending decisions. Mitigation: use WAON score as a supplementary layer on top of mandatory CIC check, not as a replacement. Privacy risk: Decree 13/2023 requires explicit consent for data use; ensure WAON T&Cs cover credit scoring use case.

First Action Step: Request from ABC Vietnam IT a data extract of all WAON member transaction records (anonymized) for the past 18 months. Set up a data science sprint to build the initial segmentation model within 3 weeks.

Session 2: Disbursement & Repayment UX

Context Briefing

Vietnam's payment landscape is undergoing rapid transformation: VietQR transactions grew 81% YoY, MoMo has 51M users, and cashless transactions hit 5.5 billion in Q1 2025 alone. Yet paradoxically, COD still dominates e-commerce at 67%, and auto-debit penetration remains extremely low. ABC's current disbursement is slow (bank transfer, sometimes 24-48 hours) and repayment options are limited. The question is not about matching competitors' payment integrations -- every FinCo will eventually have VietQR and e-wallet connections. The question is: what 10x improvement in disbursement and repayment experience can ABC create that leverages its unique ABC retail infrastructure?

Key Questions Explored

  1. What does "instant" mean for ABC's target customer? For a factory worker in Binh Duong or a shop assistant in HCMC, "instant" means walking into ABC and walking out with goods -- not waiting for a bank transfer notification.

  2. Why is auto-debit adoption so low in Vietnam, and what can ABC do differently? Vietnamese consumers distrust auto-debit because of historical overcharging incidents and because many Mass-segment workers have irregular income timing. ABC cannot rely on auto-debit as a primary collection mechanism.

  3. Can ABC retail locations serve as repayment infrastructure? Yes -- and this is the single most differentiated UX innovation ABC can deploy.

  4. How does repayment UX affect delinquency? Research from Home Credit and FE Credit shows that 30-40% of early-stage delinquency is caused not by inability to pay but by friction in the payment process (forgetting due dates, difficulty navigating payment channels, procrastination).

Discussion and Analysis

The "Pay While You Shop" Concept

The insight that transforms ABC's collection strategy: every delinquent borrower is also an ABC shopper. ABC knows which borrowers shop at ABC because it has WAON data. If a borrower is 5 days past due and shops at Citimart on Saturday morning, ABC can trigger a gentle in-app reminder at the point of purchase: "Add VND 300K to your loan payment today?" with a one-tap QR code at the checkout.

This converts a traditionally adversarial interaction (debt collection) into a seamless, frictionless, non-confrontational moment. The customer is already spending money at ABC -- adding a loan payment is psychologically easy because the payment context is "shopping," not "debt."

No other FinCo in Vietnam can do this because no other FinCo owns the retail environment where their borrowers shop.

ABC Store Credit as Disbursement -- The Closed-Loop Model

The fastest disbursement in Vietnam's consumer finance market is currently MCredit's 2-minute API-based approval through MB Bank. But even after approval, funds take 15-60 minutes to arrive in a bank account. For a customer standing in ABC Mall who wants to buy a VND 15M washing machine, those 15-60 minutes feel like an eternity.

ABC can offer something faster: approve the installment loan in 3 minutes, credit the amount to the customer's WAON card instantly, and the customer pays at the ABC checkout register immediately. Total time from application to purchase: under 5 minutes.

This is not just faster -- it is structurally different. The disbursement never leaves the ABC ecosystem. The credit is usable only at ABC stores, which means: (a) lower fraud risk (no cash-out schemes), (b) higher purchase intent (customer is already in-store with a specific product in mind), (c) ecosystem lock-in (every loan drives ABC retail revenue).

Prioritized Recommendations

2.1 "Pay While You Shop" Repayment at ABC POS
PRIORITY 1 -- BUILD

Rationale: 30-40% of early delinquency is friction-driven, not ability-driven. Embedding repayment into the weekly ABC shopping trip removes friction entirely. ABC is the only FinCo in Vietnam that owns the retail POS where its borrowers regularly transact. This single innovation could reduce B1 delinquency by 10-15 percentage points (current B1 resolution is 54.97% vs KPI target of 59.92%).

Implementation:

  • Month 1-2: Build technical integration between ABC loan management system and ABC POS/payment system. Enable "loan payment" as a line item on ABC checkout receipts.
  • Month 3: Pilot at 3 ABC Mall locations. Customer scans QR code at checkout, selects payment amount, confirms. Payment processes alongside grocery purchase.
  • Month 4-5: Deploy at all ABC stores (malls, supermarkets, minimarts). Integrate with WAON app for reminder notifications when customer enters ABC store (geofenced push notification).
  • Month 6-12: Introduce "auto-round-up" feature: customer opts in to round up every ABC purchase to the nearest VND 10,000, with the difference applied to their loan balance. This creates passive repayment that reduces balance without conscious effort.

Quick Win vs Build: BUILD (3-month technical development, but unique sustainable advantage)

Dependencies: ABC POS system integration (requires ABC Vietnam IT cooperation), QR payment infrastructure, geofencing capability in WAON/ABC app.

Risks: Technical complexity of POS integration across multiple store formats. Mitigation: start with QR-based standalone flow (not POS-integrated) for MVP; integrate into POS in Phase 2.

First Action Step: Schedule technical scoping meeting with ABC Vietnam IT and POS vendor within 2 weeks. Define MVP requirements for QR-based repayment at checkout.

2.2 Instant ABC Store Credit Disbursement
PRIORITY 1 -- BUILD

Rationale: For installment loans originated at ABC stores, disbursement to a bank account is unnecessary friction. The customer wants to buy a product at ABC -- credit the WAON card instantly. This achieves sub-60-second disbursement (vs 15-60 minutes for bank transfers), eliminates cash-out fraud, and keeps 100% of loan proceeds within the ABC ecosystem (driving group revenue).

Implementation:

  • Month 1-3: Build WAON card credit functionality (instant balance top-up upon loan approval). Integrate with LOOMS loan disbursement workflow.
  • Month 4: Pilot at ABC Mall HCMC for electronics installment purchases. Customer applies via Sales App at the store, receives approval in 3 minutes, WAON card is credited, customer pays at register.
  • Month 5-6: Extend to all ABC retail locations. Enable for cash loan disbursement (customer can choose: bank transfer or ABC Store Credit with 1% bonus for choosing store credit).

Quick Win vs Build: BUILD (requires WAON card payment infrastructure enhancement)

Dependencies: WAON card system must support instant credit loading, ABC POS must accept WAON card as payment method for high-value transactions.

Risks: Regulatory: SBV may require that loan proceeds be disbursed to a bank account (current interpretation of Circular 43/2016 is ambiguous for closed-loop payments). Mitigation: structure as "direct merchant payment" rather than "disbursement to customer."

First Action Step: Engage ABC Financial Service (parent) legal team to assess regulatory pathway for WAON card-based loan disbursement under Vietnamese law.

2.3 Behavioral Nudge Repayment Architecture
PRIORITY 2 -- QUICK WIN

Rationale: Vietnamese consumers in the Mass segment respond strongly to social proof and loss aversion. Rather than generic "your payment is due" SMS, ABC should deploy behaviorally-calibrated repayment nudges: "94% of ABC members like you paid on time this month" (social proof), "Pay today and earn 500 WAON points" (positive incentive), or "Your ABC Member status may be affected" (loss aversion for WAON benefits).

Implementation:

  • Month 1 (Quick Win): A/B test 5 different repayment reminder messages via SMS and Zalo for customers 3 days before due date. Measure payment rate by message variant. Baseline: current generic reminder achieves approximately 55% on-time payment.
  • Month 2: Deploy winning message variants across all channels. Introduce WAON point incentive for on-time payment (100 WAON points per on-time payment = VND 1,000 cost to ABC, vs VND 50-100K collection cost per delinquent account).
  • Month 3-6: Build automated nudge sequence: Day -3 (positive reminder), Day 0 (due date reminder with WAON incentive), Day +1 (gentle follow-up), Day +3 (loss-framed message about WAON status), Day +7 (restructuring offer).

Quick Win vs Build: QUICK WIN (A/B test deployable in 2 weeks)

Dependencies: SMS/Zalo messaging capability (existing), WAON point integration for incentives, A/B testing framework.

Risks: Over-messaging may annoy customers and increase Zalo OA unsubscribe rates. Mitigation: cap total messages at 4 per payment cycle; allow customer opt-out.

First Action Step: Design 5 message variants this week. Set up A/B test in SMS platform for next payment cycle. Select 10,000 customers for test group.

2.4 Multi-Rail Disbursement: E-Wallet Priority for Target Segment
PRIORITY 2 -- BUILD

Rationale: ABC's target segment (Mass, VND 7-10M/month income) disproportionately uses e-wallets over bank transfers for daily transactions. MoMo has 51M users; ZaloPay is growing rapidly among the 25-40 demographic. Disbursement to MoMo/ZaloPay is perceived as "instant" by the customer (vs bank transfers which may take minutes to hours and require navigating a banking app to confirm receipt). Offering e-wallet disbursement as the default option for cash loans reduces perceived wait time from minutes to seconds.

Implementation:

  • Month 1-2: Integrate MoMo disbursement API. Enable "Disburse to MoMo" as primary option for cash loans under VND 20M (below direct disbursement threshold).
  • Month 3-4: Add ZaloPay disbursement. Introduce customer choice screen: "Where should we send your money? MoMo / ZaloPay / Bank Account / ABC Store Credit."
  • Month 5-6: Enable split disbursement: customer can direct 50% to MoMo for immediate use and 50% to bank account for savings.

Quick Win vs Build: BUILD (2-3 months for API integration)

Dependencies: MoMo and ZaloPay partner API agreements, KYC alignment between ABC and e-wallet providers, regulatory compliance for third-party disbursement channels.

Risks: E-wallet disbursement may be perceived as less secure than bank transfers by some customers. Mitigation: offer as option, not default; position as "fastest way to receive your funds."

First Action Step: Initiate commercial discussions with MoMo business development team. Request API documentation and integration timeline.

Session 3: Marketing & Brand Platform

Context Briefing

ABC has near-zero brand awareness in consumer finance. The ABC brand is recognized in retail (malls, supermarkets) but carries no association with lending, credit cards, or financial services. Meanwhile, FE Credit spends an estimated VND 200-400B/year on advertising and commands dominant brand recognition. ABC's marketing budget is a fraction of competitors'. The challenge: how do you build a consumer finance brand from zero when you cannot outspend the incumbents?

Key Questions Explored

  1. Should ABC build a separate brand or ride on ABC's retail brand? The team concluded: lean heavily on ABC. The retail brand carries trust, physical presence, and daily consumer touchpoints. Do not create a separate "ABC Finance" identity -- position everything as "ABC" financial services.

  2. What works in Vietnam for financial product marketing? KOL/influencer campaigns have high engagement but face SBV compliance constraints (cannot make specific return promises, cannot use testimonials implying guaranteed approval). Community-based trust building via Zalo and TikTok is more sustainable and compliant.

  3. How can ABC compete without massive paid media budgets? By converting owned media (ABC physical stores, receipts, WAON app, mall digital screens, Citimart shelf tags) into financial product awareness channels. ABC has millions of weekly customer touchpoints that cost zero in media buying.

Prioritized Recommendations

3.1 "ABC Money Corner" Physical-Digital Brand Experience
PRIORITY 1 -- BUILD

Rationale: Vietnamese consumers trust what they can see and touch. A physical presence inside ABC Malls positions ABC as a permanent, trustworthy financial institution -- not a fly-by-night lender. The "Money Corner" is not a loan sales booth (which feels predatory) but a "financial wellness service" (which feels helpful). Free credit score checks create value exchange and generate warm leads. Each mall sees 30,000-100,000 visitors per week; even a 0.5% engagement rate generates 150-500 interactions per week per location.

Implementation:

  • Month 1-2: Design and build branded kiosk prototype. Include: touchscreen for credit score check (CIC self-inquiry API), savings calculator, loan eligibility checker, WAON points balance display. Staff with 1 ABC financial advisor per location.
  • Month 3: Deploy in 3 highest-traffic ABC Malls (HCMC Tan Phu, HCMC Binh Tan, Hanoi Long Bien).
  • Month 4-6: Extend to all 9 ABC Malls. Add simplified version (QR code standee + digital screen) at Citimart and MiniStop locations.
  • Month 7-12: Introduce "ABC Financial Health Score" -- a proprietary scoring tool that combines CIC data with WAON purchase history to give customers a unique financial health assessment. Position as more comprehensive than any bank's credit check.
