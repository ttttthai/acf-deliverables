The fastest-growing segment in Vietnam consumer finance (credit cards, +72.8iFiinGroup "Vietnam Credit Card Market Report 2025.pdf" — Growth Rate% YoY) is one where ACF has zero presence. BNPL, growing at 36.5% annually toward USD 8.51BESTIMATE by 2030, is another zero. This product strategy defines the performance optimization plan for existing products, the new product roadmap (credit cards, BNPL, 2-wheeler/EV), pricing strategy, and NPL reduction approach by product.

1.2 Cash Loan Performance by Sub-Product

Standard Cash Loan (NTB - New to Bank)

Performance: FPD5+ at 11.8%, which is 3.5x the market average of 3.4%. However, sample size is very limited (9 loans observed in risk threshold analysis), making definitive conclusions premature.

Assessment: RESTRICT AND MONITOR

  • Approval rate already tightened from 81% (Jul 2025) to 30.5% (Dec 2025)
  • CICiNational Credit Information Center (CIC), State Bank of Vietnam scorecard GINI at 26-27% (acceptable but not strong discriminatory power)
  • Limited backtest sample (5,867 loansiNational Credit Information Center (CIC), State Bank of Vietnam) with low positive label rates
  • Recommendation: Maintain strict underwriting; limit NTB disbursements to <10% of total; focus on income-verified, CIC-clean applicants; introduce alternative data scoring (device fingerprint, Credolab) for thin-file NTB

ACS Cross-Sell Cash Loan

Performance: Dominant channel generating VND 61BIKIGAI PROJECTION in January 2026 (60% of total disbursements). Approval rate at 70.6% and improving (+6.6% MoM). Leverages existing ACS installment customer base with known payment history.

Assessment: GROW AGGRESSIVELY

  • Lowest risk channel due to behavioral data on existing customers
  • ACF's unique ecosystem advantage -- no competitor can replicate AEON customer cross-sell
  • Recommendation: Scale ACS cross-sell by: (1) expanding eligible AEON customer base through WAONiAEON Group Loyalty Program Documentation loyalty integration, (2) introducing tiered credit limits based on installment payment history, (3) launching pre-approved offers via push notification when mobile app launches, (4) extending cross-sell to Citimart, MiniStop, and AEON Mall customers

Cash Loan Existing (PTF Legacy)

Performance: Approval rate at 47.1% for existing PTF customers. Legacy portfolio with elevated NPL.

Assessment: MANAGE DOWN

  • Serve only best-performing existing PTF customers (behavioral score cutoff)
  • Do not acquire new customers through legacy PTF channels
  • Transition performing PTF customers to AEON-branded products over 12-18 months
  • Recommendation: Reduce PTF cash loan origination by 50% over Year 1; sunset the sub-brand

Flexible / Emergency / Loyalty Cash Loans (Future)

These sub-products do not currently exist at ACF but represent market opportunities:

1.3 Installment Loan Performance by Category

Electronics (Installment CD -- Consumer Durables)

Current State: VND 702.9B (77.3% of portfolio). Dominant product originated through national sales network (NSN) at retail partners.

Performance Issues:

  • FPD5+ at 2.0% for Installment CD (below market average of 4.3% -- encouraging)
  • But New Scheme records significantly lower B0 resolution rates (94.37% vs 98.76% for ACS scheme)
  • Dealer quality varies dramatically: Cho Lon at 18.3% FPD, MediaMart at 52.9% FPD
  • Province concentration risk: Lai Chau 25.0% FPD5, Hanoi 15.9% FPD5

Assessment: OPTIMIZE AND CLEAN

  • Suspend high-FPD dealers (Cho Lon already stopped; add MediaMart and other outliers)
  • Implement dealer-level risk scoring and monitoring
  • Expand to higher-quality electronics retailers (FPT Shop, CellphoneS) where available
  • Grow: AEON retailiAEON Vietnam Ecosystem — "Lotte vs AEON – Vietnam Ecosystem.pdf" OtherData store installment (captive network, best data, lowest CAC)
  • Restrict: Third-party dealer installment (high variance, limited data control)

2.1.4 Rewards Strategy: Cashback vs Points

Recommendation: Hybrid approach -- WAON points at AEON + cashback elsewhere

Rationale:

  • WAON points create ecosystem lock-in (redeemable only at AEON stores)
  • Cashback provides universal appeal for non-AEON spending
  • Points-based earning at AEON stores drives foot traffic back to AEON ecosystem (closed-loop flywheel)
  • Cashback rate for non-AEON spending ensures competitiveness with bank cards
  • Redemption at AEON stores has higher margin for ACF (AEON absorbs cost; ACF retains interchange income)

2.1.5 Risk Model

Application Scorecard (Credit Card):

  • Layer 1: CIC bureau score and history (mandatory)
  • Layer 2: WAON loyalty data (purchase frequency, recency, basket size, category mix, membership tenure)
  • Layer 3: ACF internal behavioral data (for cross-sell from cash/installment customers)
  • Layer 4: Device fingerprint score (Credolab)
  • Layer 5: Income estimation model (using WAON spend as proxy for verified income)

Credit Limit Assignment:

  • Initial limit: Conservative (70-80% of model recommendation)
  • Dynamic adjustment: Monthly behavioral review; automatic increase for customers with 6+ months of on-time payment and utilization >30%
  • Ecosystem bonus: +20% limit increase for AEON Co-Brand cardholders demonstrating consistent AEON ecosystem spend

Early Warning:

  • Utilization spike detection (>80% utilization with declining payment)
  • Payment pattern deterioration monitoring
  • Cross-reference with installment/cash loan payment behavior
  • External trigger monitoring (CIC score changes)
