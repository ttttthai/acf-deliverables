Executive Summary

This regulatory risk assessment evaluates ABC's exposure to compliance failures, enforcement actions, and regulatory change across its current and planned operations. ABC faces a heightened regulatory risk environment driven by: (1) significant cybersecurity and data protection gaps identified by EY's current state assessment; (2) rapid regulatory evolution including the 2024 Law on Credit Institutions, Decree 13<span style="position:relative;display:inline-block;cursor:help;margin-left:3px" title=""Law on Credit Institutions 2024i"Law on Credit Institutions 2024" — National Assembly of Vietnam" — National Assembly"Decree 13/2023iGovernment "Decree 13/2023/ND-CP" — Personal Data Protection/ND-CP" — Personal Data Protection"Executive Summary Report_v.3.1.pptx" — IT Security Assessment"Executive Summary Report_v.3.1.pptx" (AdvisorsData)">i/2023 on personal data protection, and the AML Law 2022; (3) planned expansion into credit cards and BNPL requiring new regulatory approvals; (4) the novel VPBank NEO 3-party arrangement lacking clear regulatory precedent; and (5) cross-border data flows to ABC Financial Service Japan under tightening data localization requirements. Total estimated penalty exposure across all identified gaps exceeds VND 15 billion IKIGAI ESTIMATE, with license risk classified as MEDIUM given the current gap profile. Immediate remediation is required for 7 critical gaps, with 13 additional high-priority items requiring resolution within 12 months.

1.1 Critical Gaps (Immediate Remediation Required)

GAP-001: Absence of Data Encryption for Classified Information

Regulation
Circular 09/2020/TT-NHNN, Art. 12; Decree 85/2016/ND-CP; Cybersecurity Law 2018
Finding
EY identified: "No database or data file encryption; backups are only password-protected"
Risk Category
Data Security / Regulatory Compliance
Severity
CRITICAL
Likelihood of Enforcement
HIGH -- SBV has increased inspections of IT security in banking operations since 2023
Impact if Materialized
(1) SBV enforcement action including potential operational restrictions; (2) Data breach liability under Decree 13/2023; (3) Customer data exposure affecting ~200,000+ borrowers; (4) Reputational damage
Penalty Exposure
Administrative fine: VND 50-100 million per violation (Decree 88/2019 on AML penalties) IKIGAI ESTIMATE; potential operational restrictions by SBV; Decree 13 penalties up to VND 100 million for data protection failures
Remediation
Deploy Transparent Data Encryption (TDE) for all databases; implement file-level encryption for sensitive data at rest; encrypt all backup media with AES-256; establish key management procedures
Timeline
0-3 months
Estimated Cost
VND 1-2 billion IKIGAI ESTIMATE
Source: SBV Circular 43/2016/TT-NHNN; Law on Credit Institutions 2024; ABC "SBVmeeting20260211.pdf"; IKIGAI Regulatory Analysis
