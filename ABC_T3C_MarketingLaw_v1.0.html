1. Law on Advertising 2012 (No. 16/2012/QH13) and 2025 Amendments

1.1 Overview

The Law on Advertising 2012 is the primary regulation governing advertising activities in Vietnam. It was amended in 2025 with key changes relevant to financial services.

1.2 General Advertising Principles Applicable to ACF

Truthfulness

Requirement: Advertisement contents must be truthful, accurate, and clear
ABC Implication: All interest rates, fees, and product claims must be verifiable
No damage to consumers
Law Reference: Art. 8(2)

ABC Implication: Cannot mislead borrowers about loan costs or terms
Prohibited content
Law Reference: Art. 8(7)-(9)
Requirement: No false/misleading information; no unfair comparison with competitors
ABC Implication: Cannot claim "lowest rate" without evidence
Business registration
Law Reference: Art. 12

Compliance documentation

Vietnamese language

3. Decree 91/2020/ND-CP -- Anti-Spam Compliance

3.1 Overview

Decree 91/2020/ND-CP (effective October 1, 2020) governs the fighting of spam messages, spam emails, and spam calls. It replaces Decree 90/2008 with significantly stricter requirements.

3.2 Key Definitions

Advertising message
Decree 91 Definition: SMS containing commercial content promoting goods/services
ABC Context: ABC loan promotion SMS
Advertising email
Decree 91 Definition: Email containing commercial content
ABC Context: ABC marketing emails
Advertising call
Decree 91 Definition: Phone call to promote goods/services
ABC Context: ABC telemarketing calls
Spam
Decree 91 Definition: Advertising messages/emails/calls sent without prior consent or violating Decree 91
ABC Context: Any marketing without proper consent
Consent solicitation message
Decree 91 Definition: First message requesting consent to receive advertising
ABC Context: Initial opt-in request
Do-Not-Call List
Decree 91 Definition: Register of numbers that have opted out of all advertising
ABC Context: National register maintained by VNPT

3.3 Consent Requirements (Detailed)

✗
SMS marketing
Consent Type Required: Prior express consent | How to Obtain: (1) Written consent, (2) Electronic consent via website/app, (3) Consent via reply to solicitation message | Compliance: NON-COMPLIANT
✗
Email marketing
Consent Type Required: Prior express consent | How to Obtain: (1) Written consent, (2) Checkbox on website/app, (3) Double opt-in email | Compliance: NON-COMPLIANT
✗
Telemarketing calls
Consent Type Required: Prior express consent | How to Obtain: (1) Written consent, (2) Verbal consent (recorded), (3) Electronic consent | Compliance: NON-COMPLIANT
✗
Push notifications
Consent Type Required: Prior express consent (app permission) | How to Obtain: App permission request at install/first use | Compliance: N/A

Critical finding: A general consent clause in a loan agreement (e.g., "Borrower agrees to receive communications from ACF") is insufficient under Decree 91. The decree requires:

DECREE 91 REQUIREMENTS
1
Specific consent for marketing: Separate from consent to receive service-related communications
2
Informed consent: Consumer must understand they are consenting to receive advertising
3
Voluntary consent: Consent cannot be a condition of receiving the loan
4
Documented consent: Evidence of consent must be retained
Source: Law on Advertising 2012; Law on Consumer Protection 2023; Decree 91/2020/ND-CP; IKIGAI Compliance Analysis
