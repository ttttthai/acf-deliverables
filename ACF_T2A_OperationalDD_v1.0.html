2. Business Overview

2.1 Company History: PTF to ACF

AEON Consumer Finance Vietnam (ACF) was formerly known as PTF (PPF Home Credit-affiliated entity that was subsequently acquired and rebranded). The company operates under a consumer finance company (FinCo) license issued by the State Bank of Vietnam (SBV). It was rebranded to AEON Consumer Finance as part of AEON Financial Service's (AFS) strategy to build a comprehensive consumer finance presence in Vietnam, leveraging the AEON Group retail ecosystem (AEON Mall, AEON supermarkets, MaxValu, and associated retail brands).

Key milestones:

  • Original establishment as PTF with consumer lending license
  • Acquisition by AEON Financial Service Co., Ltd. (AFS Japan)
  • Rebranding to AEON Consumer Finance (ACF)
  • Charter capital: VND 1,550 billion (as of Jan 2026)
  • Registered address: 1st Floor, 2nd Floor, No. 3 Dang Thai Than, Cua Nam Ward, Hanoi City
  • Website: www.aeonfinance.com.vn

3.5 STP Rate and Bottlenecks

Finding [HIGH]: No automated straight-through processing (STP) rate data was provided. The loan origination process currently relies on:

  • NewGen LOOMS (under migration) for application and approval workflows
  • Manual interventions for field mapping, appraisal, and disbursement
  • T24 core banking for account creation and balance management
  • Multiple integration points (CIC, FPT eKYC, FPT eSign, Product Service, Scoring) creating process friction

Key bottlenecks identified:

  • LOOMS migration incomplete; DSD Phase issue list outstanding
  • T24 limitations preventing automated loan classification
  • Manual data entry processes for installment loan origination (separate guidelines for cash loans vs installment loans)
  • eKYC and credit inquiry costs of VND 14 billion annually

4.3 Payment Processing

Payment collection operates through multiple channels:

  • Partner collection agents (third-party) with dediACF Banking Partner Agreement — Disbursement Channelicated API integration (partner management, loan query, scheduled payment, early settlement, partial prepayment)
  • Bank transfer via SeABank (primary banking partner for disbursement and collection)
  • NAPASiNAPAS — National Payment Corporation of Vietnam integration for fast payment channel
  • Direct debit arrangements
  • Collection fee processing embedded in payment flow (minimum payment amount must cover collection fees)

Finding [MEDIUM]: Payment collection relies heavily on third-party collection partners with relatively simple system integration (partniNAPAS — National Payment Corporation of Vietnamer information entry is a basic manual process). The disbursement architecture has multiple fallback channels (SeABank direct, CITAD, NAPAS fast) suggesting operational resilience but also complexity.
