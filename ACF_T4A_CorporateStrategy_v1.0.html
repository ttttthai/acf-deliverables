1. Strategic Assessment

1.1 Current Strategy Review

ABC's current strategy, inherited from the PTF acquisition and guided by AFS Japan, rests on three pillars:

  1. Cash Loan Core: PTF's legacy cash loan book (VND 137.8B) serves as the primary revenue engine, supplemented by ACS cross-sell (the dominant disbursement channel at 60% of total volume).
  2. Installment Expansion: Installment consumer durables (VND 702.9B) represent 77.3% of the lending portfolio, with a national sales network across retail partners.
  3. Pipeline Products: Credit cards, BNPL, and 2-wheeler loans are in various stages of planning but none have launched.

Assessment: The current strategy is fundamentally sound in its direction (product diversification, ecosystem leverage, digital transformation) but critically deficient in execution velocity. While competitors launched BNPL in 2021, credit cards in 2020, and EV financing in 2022, ABC's credit card program is not expected until 2027-2028. The 2-3 year execution lag against competitors represents the single greatest strategic risk.
