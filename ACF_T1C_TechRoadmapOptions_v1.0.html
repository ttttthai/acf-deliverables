3. Option A: Conservative -- Stay on T24 R25 + Enhance LOOMS

3.1 Strategy

Minimize technology disruption by enhancing the existing stack. Keep T24 R25 as core banking, upgrade LOOMS with API layer and digital front-end, and add bolt-on systems for credit card (Way4 enhancement) and collection.

3.2 Scope of Work

Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-6)

  • Implement EY infrastructure recommendations (private cloud migration, DR setup)
  • Deploy cybersecurity stack: SIEM, MFA, PAM, WAF
  • Add API gateway layer on top of T24 and LOOMS
  • Integrate VNPT eKYC with LOOMS
  • Deploy basic digital application form (web-based)
  • Implement NAPASiNAPAS — National Payment Corporation of Vietnam 24/7 and VietQRiNAPAS "VietQR Integration Standards" for payment collection

Phase 2: Product Enhancement (Months 7-12)

  • Enhance LOOMS for 2-wheeler loan product configuration
  • Implement basic BNPL workflow on LOOMS (installment at POS)
  • Deploy collection system (Credgenics or equivalent)
  • Launch customer mobile app (basic: view balance, make payments)
  • Integrate CICiNational Credit Information Center (CIC), State Bank of Vietnam automated credit check

Phase 3: Credit Card (Months 13-18)

  • Extend Way4 card processing (requires VPBank/TSA negotiation)
  • Build credit card origination flow on enhanced LOOMS
  • Implement card embossing and delivery workflow
  • Launch credit card product (MVP with limited features)

3.4 Pros

  • Lowest disruption to ongoing operations
  • Leverages existing team skills and knowledge
  • Shortest time to partial improvements
  • No core banking migration risk
  • Lowest upfront investment

3.5 Cons

  • Does not address fundamental architectural limitations
  • Credit card launch depends on Way4/VPBank TSA relationship
  • Limited scalability for high-volume products (BNPL)
  • Technical debt continues to accumulate
  • eKYC and digital experience will remain inferior to competitors
  • Cannot achieve real-time processing for new products
  • Difficulty attracting modern tech talent

3.6 Key Risks

  • Way4 Dependency: Credit card launch blocked if VPBank TSA does not extend card processing
  • LOOMS Limitations: Enhancing a system with known security vulnerabilities may be unsustainable
  • Competitive Gap: Market leaders (Home Credit, FE Credit, Cake) continue to advance while ABC patches
  • Talent Retention: T24/Oracle skills becoming scarce; developers prefer modern stacks

4. Option B: Moderate -- Upgrade to Transact + Replace LOOMS

4.1 Strategy

Upgrade T24 R25 to Temenos Transact for improved API capabilities, replace LOOMS with a modern LOS (PISMO Lending or MambuiVendor Assessment — IKIGAI Technology Research LOS), and implement PISMO for credit card processing. This is a modular transformation that addresses the most critical gaps while managing migration risk.

4.2 Scope of Work

Phase 1: Infrastructure & Digital Foundation (Months 1-6)

  • Complete EY infrastructure recommendations (private cloud, cybersecurity)
  • Deploy PISMO for ciNAPAS — National Payment Corporation of Vietnamredit card issuing and piNAPAS "VietQR Integration Standards"rocessing
  • Implement VNPT eKYC + HyperVerge
  • Build customer mobile app with API-first architecture
  • Deploy NAPAS 24/7, VietQR, eWallet integrations
  • Implement Credgenics for digital collections

Phase 2: Core Enhancement + Credit Card Launch (Months 7-12)

  • Begin T24 R25 to Transact upgrade (parallel run)
  • Launch credit card product via PISMO (debit/credit/prepaid)
  • Implement PISMO Digital Lending for BNPL product
  • Deploy alternative credit scoring (Experian + Credolab)
  • Launch 2-wheeler loan product on enhanced LOS
  • Implement data lake (Medallion Architecture per EY)

Phase 3: Full Transact + Optimization (Months 13-24)

  • Complete Transact upgrade and cutover
  • Migrate existing loan book operations to Transact APIs
  • Implement AI/ML credit scoring models
  • Deploy Voice AI for collections (FPT.AI)
  • Full mobile app with self-service capabilities
  • Advanced analytics and real-time decisioning

5. Option C: Aggressive -- Migrate to Cloud-Native (Mambu/PISMO)

5.1 Strategy

Full core banking migration from T24 to a cloud-native platform (Mambu recommended, with PISMO for cards). This is the most ambitious option, aligned with what Cake by VPBank and TNEX have achieved, but carries the highest execution risk for an organization in ABC's current financial position.

5.2 Scope of Work

Phase 1: Cloud Foundation + Quick Wins (Months 1-9)

  • Deploy Mambu as new core banking platform on AWS
  • Implement PISMO for credit card issuing and processing
  • Build new customer mobile app (React Native or Flutter)
  • Integrate full eKYC stack (VNPT + HyperVerge)
  • Launch credit card product (first product on new stack)
  • Deploy NAPAS, VietQR, eWallet integrations
  • Implement Credgenics collections

Phase 2: Loan Products Migration (Months 10-18)

  • Configure cash loan product on Mambu
  • Configure installment loan product on Mambu
  • Launch BNPL product natively on Mambu
  • Launch 2-wheeler loan with IoT integration
  • Begin data migration from T24 (existing loan book)
  • Deploy AI/ML scoring platform
  • Implement real-time analytics (Databricks/Snowflake)

Phase 3: Cutover + Optimization (Months 19-30)

  • Complete data migration from T24
  • Decommission T24 and LOOMS
  • Full digital collections with Voice AI
  • Advanced personalization engine (Clever.AI or similar)
  • Open Banking APIs for partner integrations
  • Continuous optimization and feature releases
