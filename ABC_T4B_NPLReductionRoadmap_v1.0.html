1.2 Root Cause Deep Dive: CashLoan NTB

Problem: FPD5+ at 11.8% is 3.5x the market average of 3.4%.

Root Causes:

  1. Insufficient scoring power: CIC scorecard GINI at 26-27% provides only "acceptable" discriminatory power. For NTB customers with no ABC internal history, the model cannot distinguish good from bad with enough precision.
  2. Limited alternative data: No device fingerprint scoring, no telco data integration, no social data overlay to supplement thin CICiNational Credit Information Center (CIC), State Bank of Vietnam files.
  3. Income verification gaps: Approval for customers with unverified income leads to over-extension of credit relative to repayment capacity.
  4. Channel quality: Some NTB origination channels (specific NSN agents) may be driving adverse selection.
  5. Small sample issue: Only 9 loans in the FPD5+ analysis sampleiNational Credit Information Center (CIC), State Bank of Vietnam, making statistical conclusions unreliable but directionally concerning.

Remediation Actions:

  • Immediately restrict NTB cash loan origination to CIC Group 1 customers with verified income only
  • Deploy Credolab device fingerprint scoring as first alternative data source (SDK integration, 2-4 weeks)
  • Implement income estimation model using e-commerce/telco spend patterns for thin-file applicants
  • Introduce mandatory 48-hour cooling-off period for NTB cash loan applications (reduce impulse borrowing)
  • Reduce maximum NTB loan amount from current levels to VND 10M until scoring improves
  • Target: Reduce NTB FPD5+ from 11.8% to below 5% within 6 months

1.3 Root Cause Deep Dive: Installment TW

Problem: FPD5+ at 9.5% is 3.5x the market average of 2.7%.

Root Causes:

  1. Vehicle-specific risk: Certain 2-wheeler models attract higher-risk borrowers. No vehicle-level risk differentiation in current scoring.
  2. Dealer influence: Dealers may incentivize applications from marginal borrowers to earn commissions.
  3. Down payment adequacy: LTV may be too high for this segment, reducing borrower skin-in-the-game.
  4. Seasonal/geographic concentration: TW lending concentrated in provinces with higher default rates.

Remediation Actions:

  • Require minimum 20% down payment for all TW loans (increase skin-in-the-game)
  • Implement dealer-level risk scoring: dealers with FPD above threshold get suspended
  • Introduce vehicle-level risk adjustment (EV vs ICE; new vs used; brand tier)
  • Restrict TW lending in provinces with >10% FPD5+ until geographic scoring is refined
  • Target: Reduce TW FPD5+ from 9.5% to below 4% within 6 months

1.4 Root Cause Deep Dive: Installment CD (Dealer Quality)

Problem: New Scheme B0 resolution at 94.37%IKIGAI PROJECTION vs 98.76% for ACS scheme. Specific dealers showing extreme FPD.

Root Causes:

  1. Dealer moral hazard: Cho Lon (18.3% FPD, 381 cases) and MediaMart (52.9% FPD, 141 cases) demonstrate that some dealers are originating fraudulent or extremely low-quality applications.
  2. Inadequate dealer vetting: No systematic dealer scoring or monitoring framework.
  3. New Scheme design flaws: The new installment product scheme records lower B0 resolution, suggesting product design or customer selection differences from the proven ACS scheme.
  4. Geographic concentration: Central region (28.6% of new loans) and HCMC (23.4%) show varying quality.

Remediation Actions:

  • Immediately suspend all dealers with FPD5+ above 10% (Cho Lon already stopped; add MediaMart and others)
  • Implement dealer performance monitoring dashboard with monthly FPD tracking
  • Introduce dealer tiering: Tier 1 (best) = full credit; Tier 2 (acceptable) = restricted credit; Tier 3 (poor) = suspended
  • Review New Scheme product design against ACS scheme to identify and fix risk driver differences
  • Require field verification for all dealer-originated loans above VND 20M
  • Target: Reduce Installment CD FPD5+ below 3% (industry benchmark) within 6 months

1.5 Root Cause Deep Dive: Collection Effectiveness Decline

Problem: B1 resolution rate declined from 88% (April 2025) to 54.97% (January 2026). B1-B3 roll rates accelerating. 60% of B4+ portfolio uncovered by staff.

Root Causes:

  1. Contact rate crisis: B1 contact and connection rate at only 7.3% despite 18 calls per contract. This means 92.7% of delinquent customers cannot be reached.
  2. Data quality: Phone numbers and addresses on file are outdated or incorrect for a large portion of the delinquent portfolio.
  3. Understaffing: B1-B3 buckets have only 2 staff per bucket -- critically understaffed for the volume of delinquent accounts.
  4. Limited channels: Collection relies primarily on phone calls. No automated SMS, push notification, Zalo, or digital self-service payment channels.
  5. B4+ coverage gap: 65K cases (VND 1,377BIKIGAI PROJECTION POS) in B4+ are "Not Covered" -- no staff assigned. This is the single largest collection gap.
  6. Field collector productivity: Field collectors average only VND 15M/month in collections, far below legal call team at VND 76M/month.

Remediation Actions: (Detailed in Section 5: Collection Optimization)
