Executive Summary

This document defines 5 detailed customer personas representing ABC's current and target customer segments, along with current-state and future-state journey maps for key product flows. The personas are derived from operational data (loan portfolio composition, channel mix, approval rates, demographic indicators) and strategic direction (BNPL targeting Gen Z, agent banking for underbanked, credit card cross-sell for loyal customers). Journey maps identify 23 moments of truth, 15 critical pain points, and 18 pain-to-opportunity conversions that inform ABC's digital transformation priorities. IKIGAI ILLUSTRATIVE

VND 15M (variable, net)

High school + vocational (cosmetology)
Household
Married; 1 child (6); owns house with mortgage
Banking
Techcombank personal; mostly cash business
Credit / AEON
CIC Group 1; Home Credit alumni; WAON 3 mo

Goals

  1. Expand her salon business (add 2 more chairs, new equipment -- VND 80-100M needed)
  2. Manage cash flow gaps (clients pay irregularly; rent and supplies are fixed costs)
  3. Build a savings cushion for her child's education
  4. Eventually wants a credit card for business expenses and online purchasing

Pain Points IKIGAI ILLUSTRATIVE

  1. Income verification difficulty: Self-employed income is variable and hard to document; bank statements don't reflect full income (cash-heavy business)
  2. High rejection risk: CashLoan NTB approval rate is only 30.5%; self-employed applicants face even tighter scrutiny
  3. No flexible repayment: Current products have rigid monthly schedules; her income fluctuates seasonally (Tet, holidays = high; mid-year = lower)
  4. Limited loan amounts: Needs VND 80-100M but unsecured cash loans may cap lower for her risk profile
  5. No relationship recognition: Despite being a Home Credit alumni with clean history, ABC treats her as "new to bank"
  6. eKYC friction: CCCD NFC chip reading failed on her older phone; had to visit a branch for identity verification

Digital Behavior

  • Smartphone: iPhone 11 (used, bought secondhand); uses WiFi at salon; limited mobile data
  • Daily apps: Zalo (business communication with clients), Facebook (salon marketing page), TikTok (content for salon), bank apps
  • Social media: Facebook groups for beauty professionals; Zalo groups for local business owners
  • Financial behavior: Receives payments via bank transfer and cash; uses Zalo Pay occasionally; saves in bank fixed deposits
  • Discovery: Word-of-mouth from other business owners; Facebook groups; local advertising
  • Trust signals: Prefers brands recommended by friends; values customer service responsiveness

Journey Touchpoints IKIGAI ILLUSTRATIVE

  • Awareness: Friend (another salon owner) mentions AEON cash loan; sees Zalo ad
  • Consideration: Calls ABC hotline to ask about loan terms; asks about documentation requirements
  • Application: Provides CCCD, household registration, bank statements (3 months); eKYC process
  • Approval: Manual review required (self-employed); takes 2-3 business days
  • Disbursement: Bank transfer to Techcombank account; 1-2 days after approval
  • Repayment: Sets calendar reminder; manual bank transfer; occasionally late due to cash flow
  • Engagement: Receives collection calls if late; no proactive relationship management

Dream Experience

"I want ABC to understand that my income is real even if it doesn't show perfectly in bank statements. I'd love to upload photos of my business, show my client bookings, and have the system recognize that I'm a reliable small business owner. I need flexibility -- maybe let me pay more in good months and less in slow months. And don't call me at work if I'm one day late; send me a Zalo message with a payment link I can tap."

Goals

  1. Finance his older child's high school tuition and school supplies (VND 15-20M/year)
  2. Buy a new refrigerator and washing machine for the family (VND 15-25M total)
  3. Have access to emergency cash when unexpected expenses arise (medical, vehicle repair)
  4. Eventually buy a small piece of land in his home province for retirement

Pain Points IKIGAI ILLUSTRATIVE

  1. Thin credit file: Never had a formal loan; CICiNational Credit Information Center (CIC), State Bank of Vietnam has no record; traditional scoring models reject him
  2. Low digital literacy: Can use basic VPBank NEO features (balance check, transfer) but struggles with complex app flows
  3. Language and documentation: Official financial documents are intimidating; prefers simple Vietnamese explanations
  4. Limited time: Works 6 days/week, 10-12 hours/day; cannot visit branches during business hours
  5. Informal lending dependence: Currently borrows from informal lenders (vay nong) at 5-10%/month for emergencies
  6. No smartphone sophistication: Uses a Xiaomi Redmi 9A (budget phone); apps must be lightweight and simple
  7. Trust issues: Wary of financial institutions due to stories of hidden fees and aggressive collection

Digital Behavior

  • Smartphone: Xiaomi Redmi 9A (budget Android); WiFi at factory during breaks; prepaid mobile data (limited)
  • Daily apps: Zalo (family communication), VPBank NEO (salary check), YouTube (entertainment during breaks)
  • Social media: Facebook (passive; scrolls news feed); Zalo groups for factory workers
  • Financial behavior: Cash for most transactions; VPBank NEO for salary receipt and occasional transfers; no e-wallet
  • Discovery: Co-worker recommendations; factory bulletin board; VPBank NEO in-app notifications
  • Trust signals: Trusts his factory's HR department; trusts VPBank because factory uses it; ABC Mall is familiar

Journey Touchpoints IKIGAI ILLUSTRATIVE

  • Awareness: VPBank NEO push notification about ABC loan availability; co-worker mentions it
  • Consideration: Asks HR department if it's legitimate; checks VPBank NEO interface for details
  • Application: Attempts application in VPBank NEO during lunch break; needs help from younger co-worker for eKYC
  • Approval: Uncertain about status; doesn't know where to check; waits for a call
  • Disbursement: Money arrives in VPBank account; checks on VPBank NEO
  • Repayment: Auto-debit from VPBank salary account (preferred); needs clear SMS confirmation each month
  • Engagement: Minimal; appreciates being left alone if payments are current

Dream Experience

"I just want it to be simple. I open VPBank NEO, I see 'You can borrow up to VND 20M'. I tap a button, scan my CCCD, take a selfie, and it's done. The money goes into my VPBank account by tomorrow. Every month they take the payment automatically from my salary. I don't want to think about it. And if something happens and I need to delay one payment, I want to send a message on Zalo and have someone understand my situation."

  4. Maximize rewards and cashback on everyday spending
  5. Never wants to feel "in debt" -- prefers short-term installments with clear end dates

Pain Points IKIGAI ILLUSTRATIVE

  1. No awareness of ACF: Has never heard of ABC Consumer Finance; associates AEON with shopping, not finance
  2. Traditional finance aversion: Perceives consumer finance companies as "for poor people" or "predatory"; prefers fintech brands
  3. Instant gratification expectation: Expects Shopee SPayLater-level speed; anything requiring >2 minutes feels broken
  4. No interest in calling anyone: Will not call a hotline; will abandon any process requiring a phone call
  5. Privacy conscious: Concerned about data sharing; reads app permissions; will not install apps that request excessive access
  6. Visual and social proof dependent: Needs to see TikTok reviews, Instagram stories, and friend recommendations before trusting a financial product
  7. Small ticket need: Most purchases are VND 500K-5M; traditional VND 20M+ loan products are oversized for her needs

Digital Behavior

  • Smartphone: iPhone 14 (latest generation); always on WiFi or 5G; mobile-first exclusively
  • Daily apps: TikTok (3+ hours), Instagram, Shopee, MoMo, Grab, TNEX, Netflix, Spotify
  • Social media: TikTok (primary), Instagram (visual), Twitter/X (news), Facebook (decreasing)
  • Financial behavior: SPayLater on Shopee; MoMo for bill payments; TNEX for saving pockets; Apple Pay via TNEX card
  • Discovery: TikTok creator recommendations; Instagram stories from friends; app store discovery; never searches Google for finance
  • Trust signals: Values modern UI/UX; trusts brands with strong social media presence; influenced by peer usage; avoids anything that looks "old" or "corporate"

Journey Touchpoints IKIGAI ILLUSTRATIVE

  • Awareness: TikTok creator shows "AEON PayLater" at ABC Mall checkout; Instagram ad for AEON credit card
  • Consideration: Checks App Store reviews; asks friends on Zalo group if they've used it; looks for TikTok reviews
  • Application: Downloads AEON app; expects to sign up with phone number + OTP in <30 seconds
  • Activation: Wants BNPL activated immediately at checkout without a separate application process
  • First Purchase: Scans QR at ABC Mall fashion store; selects "Pay in 3 months - 0%"; biometric confirm
  • Repayment: Auto-debit from TNEX account; push notification 3 days before due date; tap to pay in app
  • Engagement: Wants personalized offers via push notification; WAON points for on-time payment; Instagram-worthy card design

Dream Experience

"I'm shopping at ABC Mall, I see a jacket I love for VND 2M. I open the AEON app, scan a QR code at checkout, and it instantly shows me: 'Pay VND 667K x 3 months, 0% interest.' I tap my Face ID, done. I walk out with my jacket. Three weeks later I get a push notification: 'You've been pre-approved for an AEON Credit Card with 3% cashback at AEON -- tap to get your virtual card instantly.' The card has a cool design I can share on Instagram. That's how finance should work."

  • Trust signals: Trusts ABC brand (Japanese quality); trusts recommendations from colleagues; values transparency in pricing

  • Approval: Expects instant approval given her history; would be frustrated by re-verification
  • Card Issuance: Wants virtual card instantly in app; physical card delivered to home
  • Usage: ABC Mall shopping with enhanced WAON earning (3x points); online shopping; auto-pay utility bills
  • Engagement: Monthly WAON points summary; birthday offers; upgrade opportunities; financial wellness content

I tap to accept, my virtual card appears, and I use it immediately at ABC Mall that weekend. My WAON points from shopping and from card spending combine into one balance. At the end of the month, I see everything -- my loan, my card, my points -- in one screen.
