2.2 Financial Performance Trends (2022-2025)

FE Credit:

  • 2022-2023: Deep losses due to NPL surge post-COVID; provision charges consumed income
  • 2024: Returned to breakeven; NPL declined to ~6%IKIGAI PROJECTION range
  • H1 2025: PBT grew 113% YoY, 79% QoQ; CIR reduced to 30%
  • Strategy: Cross-sell to existing customers; credit card issuance; behavioral risk-based pricing
  • Technology: Digital lending platform, PegaiFE Credit "Pega_Collection_System_Description.docx" OtherData/FE Credit IT Docs collections, Experian scoring
  • FiFiinGroup "Vietnam Consumer Finance 1H2025 Review.pdf" — Home Credit NPLE Credit contributes VND 6,107B TOI YTD to VPBank (36% YoY growth)

Home Credit Vietnam:

  • 2024: Profit after tax VND 1,291B (3.44x vs. prior year)
  • NPL: 1.76% (down from 2.49% in 2023); industry best
  • Problem debt (Groups 2-5): 6.5% (down from 11.3% in 2022)
  • Total assets grew 7%; credit outstanding up 12.4%
  • Acquired by SCBX (Thai conglomerate) in 2024
  • FiinRatings A with stable outlook -- highest among FinCos
  • Strategy: Digital-first origination; AI-powered collections; diversified product mix

HD Saison:

  • H1 2025: PBT VND 709B (18% YoY increase)
  • Target FY2025: PBT VND 1,500BIKIGAI PROJECTION
  • Strategy: Joint venture advantage with HDBank (low-cost funding)
  • Strong POS lending network via electronics/motorcycle dealers
  • Stable NPL in 4-5% range

MCredit:

  • H1 2025: PBT VND 60BIKIGAI PROJECTION (exceeding full-year 2024 profit of VND 51BIKIGAI PROJECTION)
  • Recovery from prolonged adjustment period
  • Strategy: Leveraging MB Bank customer base for cross-sell

VietCredit:

  • H1 2025: PBT VND 318B (vs. loss of VND 185B in H1 2024)
  • Dramatic turnaround; credit card focus paying off
  • Listed company (HoSE); market scrutiny on performance
