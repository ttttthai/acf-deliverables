1. Regulatory Framework -- Data Protection and Cybersecurity

1.1 Decree 13/2023/ND-CP -- Personal Data Protection

1.1.1 Overview

Decree 13/2023/ND-CP, issued April 17, 2023 and effective July 1, 2023, is Vietnam's foundational personal data protection regulation. It applies to all organizations (domestic and foreign) that process personal data of individuals in Vietnam.

Cross-border transfer requirements under Decree 13:

  1. The data controller must conduct a cross-border data transfer impact assessment documenting: the purpose and necessity of transfer, the data types transferred, the data protection capabilities of the receiving country, the contractual safeguards in place.
  2. The impact assessment must be filed with the Ministry of Public Security (Department of Cybersecurity and High-tech Crime Prevention) before the transfer commences.
  3. The data controller must obtain explicit consent from data subjects for the cross-border transfer, specifying the receiving country and entity.
  4. The data controller must ensure the receiving entity provides adequate data protection measures.
  5. Data localization: Under the Cybersecurity Law 2018i"Cybersecurity Law 2018" — National Assembly of Vietnam (Art. 26) and Decree 53/2022, certain data must be stored within Vietnam. The SBV meeting (Feb 2026) confirmed that CIF (Customer Information File) data must be maintained locally in Vietnam (Viettel IDC) even when using overseas CMS SaaS.

1.3 Circular 09/2020/TT-NHNN -- Banking IT Security

1.3.1 Scope and Applicability

Circular 09/2020/TT-NHNN sets minimum requirements for information system security in banking operations. It applies to credit institutions (including consumer finance companies like ABC), foreign bank branches, intermediary payment service providers, and credit information companies.
