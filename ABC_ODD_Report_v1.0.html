ABC is a consumer finance company undergoing significant transformation while simultaneously managing critical operational, risk, and technology challenges. The company possesses a fundamentally sound strategic direction -- leveraging the ABC Group ecosystem for product diversification and digital transformation -- but execution is lagging by 2-3 years relative to competitors. The overall ODD risk rating is AMBER, driven by:

  • RED areas: Cybersecurity posture, NPL trajectory, absence of digital customer channels, data protection non-compliance
  • AMBER areas: Technology infrastructure, loan classification automation, collection effectiveness, AML monitoring, marketing compliance

2. Company Overview

2.1 History: PTF to ACF

ABC Consumer Finance Vietnam (ABC) was formerly known as PTF (Post and Telecom Finance Company). ABC Financial Service Co., Ltd. (AFS Japan) fully acquired PTF in 2025, rebranding it as ABC Consumer Finance as part of AFS's strategy to build a comprehensive consumer finance presence in Vietnam leveraging the ABC Group retail ecosystem. Key milestones include the original PTF establishment with SBV consumer lending license, full acquisition by AFS Japan (100% ownership), rebranding to ABC, charter capital raise to VND 1,550 billion, and ACS Trading Vietnam (installment payments, 700,000 members) operating alongside as the cross-sell engine.

2.2 Ownership Structure

ABC Financial Service Co., Ltd. (AFS Japan) holds 100% ownership. AFS provides strategic oversight, capital injections, risk management frameworks, and technology governance through a Steering Committee (Stecomi) structure with active participation from senior AFS personnel. The ABC Group operates 8 subsidiaries in Vietnam creating a comprehensive retail and services ecosystem.

2.3 SBV License and Regulatory Standing

ABC holds an active SBV consumer finance company license covering lending activities (cash loans, installment loans). Credit card issuance is under development with SBV pre-consultation completed February 11, 2026. Insurance distribution is active through partners. The company maintains regular SBV reporting (monthly, quarterly), CICiNational Credit Information Center (CIC), State Bank of Vietnam R18 classification integration, and STRAVIS reporting to AFS Group. FY2025 financial audit is on track with final report expected February 28, 2026.
